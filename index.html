Vascular Audit Database System

Vascular Audit Database System (VADS) is a desktop MS-Access application, designed by a vascular surgeon for vascular surgeons.

As the scope and complexity of all medical and surgical interventions increases it behoves all medical professionals to regularly review and audit their outcomes in the particular specialty in which they practice. This project is designed to facilitate these aims and to allow for detailed collection of data for all patients being admitted under the care of vascular surgeons. This data can then be correlated to produce an audit of both the scope and complexity of the practice as well as key performance indicators associated with the various procedures. It will also allow for clinical based research. With these in mind a software package has been developed which will allow the collection and collation of data on patients attending the vascular department. The software is based on but not exclusive to the fields collected by

  • The National Vascular Registry
  • The Society for Vascular Surgery Vascular Quality Initiative
  • and the reporting standards for the various index vascular surgical procedure published in the Journal of Vascular Surgery

The index procedures in vascular surgery for which patients are admitted are recorded as follows:-
  • Abdominal Aortic Aneurysm (open, EVAR, and complex)
  • Carotid Endarterectomy
  • Lower Limb Revascularization open surgical revascularization and endovascular revascularization
  • Upper limb revascularization
  • Trauma both the iatrogenic and non-iatrogenic
  • Amputation (minor and major)
  • Management of vascular wounds
  • Access Procedures for dialysis, ECMO, et cetera
  • Other Miscellaneous Vascular Procedures

New admissions are labelled as AAA | Access | Amputation | CEA | LLR | Miscellaneous | Trauma | ULR | Veins or Wound.

The data is collected in each of the following 6 sections :

  1. Mode of Admission i.e. elective/emergency and source
  2. Pre-operative Assessment including risk factor assessment preoperative diagnostic tests and anaesthetic assessment
  3. Details of the procedure(s) performed as well as additional procedures or procedures required to treat complications
  4. Post-operative recovery, length of stay, type of post-operative recovery bed, and discharge whether to step down or usual place of residence
  5. Initial post-operative in hospital complications if any and the outcome of such complications as well as post-operative morbidity and mortality
  6. Long-term follow-up

Section 3, 'Details of the procedure(s)', contains a wide collection of data elements, reflecting the complexity of the relevant surgical procedure.
The other sections contain many data items common to all admissions together with extra data items and coding standards for specific admissions.

Thus in all groups indicated above the data elements collected will be in line with the standards on which the registry is based as indicated in the overview above. This will include basic biographic data, mode of admission, referring specialty, and if the admission was emergency or elective and the indication there for.
For example in the case of abdominal aortic aneurysm was the admission elective or for a symptomatic or ruptured aneurysm.
In the case of lower limb revascularisation was the admission for intermittent claudication or critical limb ischaemia.
In the case of carotid endarterectomy was the carotid symptomatic or asymptomatic et cetera et cetera.
In terms of preoperative assessment data similar data fields as indicated in the overview above will be collected. Again these will include indication for admission and intervention, risk factor assessment, comorbidities, smoking history, diagnostic tests, anaesthetic assessment, ASA grade, et cetera et cetera.
For example in the case of abdominal aortic aneurysm the indication for intervention will be recorded, date and size of aneurysm when first seen, date and size when threshold was reached, CT scan result, MDT result and outcome, and any other complicating factors. The precise details of the procedure performed will be recorded including for example in the case of aortic aneurysm open repair:- duration of procedure, blood loss, graft used, proximal and distal anastomosis type, et cetera et cetera in line with the standards indicated above. Similar comprehensive fields will be recorded for EVAR. This comprehensive template will be used for all the index procedures for example in lower limb revascularisation the TASC classification of the occlusive lesions will be used and the precise proximal and distal anastomosis for bypass will be recorded as will the conduit et cetera et cetera.
